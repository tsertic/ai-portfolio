The Condensed Version

There's a particular kind of satisfaction in making old systems do new tricks. By day, I work at Arctis d.o.o., where I wrestle with enterprise-grade Archibus deployments for organizations that don't tolerate downtime—Saudi Aramco, the European Space Agency, the European Court of Justice, the University of Luxembourg, among others. The job spans the full absurd spectrum: Java backends, SQL surgery, React grafted onto legacy codebases, server orchestration with Tomcat, and documentation that someone might actually read.


Outside the enterprise bubble, I build Next.js + TypeScript sites for clients and friends, tinker with AI experiments, and write utility scripts that save me from doing the same thing twice. I'm driven by the puzzle—whether that means resurrecting a decade-old system or spinning up something from nothing. When the screen goes dark, you'll find me playing basketball, cycling, or losing at chess. Occasionally I resurface to walk Eevee the dog or convince Anja that debugging is a life skill.
